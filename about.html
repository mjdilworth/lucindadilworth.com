About

My interest in art began as a child, from my constant surrounding of artists. However, it flourished at the beginning of Covid-19 in 2020 by reintroducing myself to watercolors, guided by Greek Artist Maria Stini. Watercolors allowed me to break the boundaries and explore my emotions as well as social issues through the process of creating images. These artworks encompass; George Orwell's book 1984 in relation to current affairs and my passion for skiing by incorporating body movement and breaking down the human body, leading to my fascination with surrealism. I naturally began to evolve the vision of my unconscious mind of the higher power figures I constructed, such as "Moon Lady" and "Ladyshroom."

My natural progression to evolve my designs, sketches, and ideas led to the beginning of my exploration into digital art through the ability to display stories and thoughts, which coincide with my philosophy that art is a journey, and the outcome is uncertain. I believe that art holds no expectations; it is an outlet for the present emotion.

I have independently been exploring Repousse and developing control in Flame Painting with copper intending to create more dimensional pieces and hone in on the fluidity that coincides with watercolor.

In the future, I aim to combine all skill sets to create an immersive experience, incorporating projection mapping, Augmented reality, and physical artworks to create an environment of the old greek world, projecting works of my great grandfather to display and commemorate his work for the Greek National Bank.
