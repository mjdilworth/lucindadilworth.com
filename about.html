About

Lucinda Dilworth, a digital artist following her Greek origins, is aiming to evolve as an artist to suit the modern and technological-driven world.

Lucinda is a recent graduate from the University of Colorado, Boulder, and is passionate about how digital mediums and entrepreneurial drive intertwine. Coming from a diverse background - half Greek and half British - and, with her formative years spent in Athens, Greece, her experiences and cultural heritage infuse her work with a unique and compelling perspective.

Alongside her competitive alpine skiing background, she now takes center stage as the curator and artist behind the captivating collection "Artificial Impression," where she harnesses the power of technology and digital art to create an immersive experience that challenges perceptions to leave a lasting impression.

Her fusion of creativity, innovative thinking, and a deep understanding of the digital landscape sets Lucinda apart and drives her to push boundaries and explore the intricate relationship between art, technology, and artificial intelligence.

Lucinda is an artist at the forefront of the digital art revolution. Her artistic journey began with the creation of digital paintings that serve as the canvas for her visionary animations. These animations come alive in a world where technology seamlessly blends with art. Her expertise lies in transforming these digital masterpieces into immersive experiences through the magic of augmented reality, projection mapping, and live visual editing. She invites her audience to step beyond the confines of traditional art, immersing themselves in her vibrant digital realms where creativity knows no bounds. In her hands, art becomes an ever-evolving journey, a fusion of the digital and the tangible, where each piece tells a story and leaves an indelible impression on the senses.

Artificial Impression:

The "Artificial Impression" collection draws inspiration from George Orwell's dystopian masterpiece, "1984," and explores the complex relationship between technology and human experience. Through the integration of artificial intelligence, digital art, visual effects, animation, and projection mapping, the collection of artworks reflects the themes of surveillance, control, and the manipulation of reality portrayed in the novel. "Artificial Impression" serves as a captivating exploration of the ever-evolving role of technology in society and its potential impact on individual freedom and expression, echoing the haunting warnings of "1984" while pushing the boundaries of artistic innovation.
